Search Intent

User often have very different expectations when using search engines, and in it guidelines, goggle takes a lot about how to understand what users are looking for when using different queries. Providing exhaustive answers to user queries incrases the chances of your content being rated and quaility.

Search intent can be broken down into four distinct types:

  • Informational - the searcher is looking for specific information on a topic.
  • Navigational - the searcher is looking for specific web page or site.
  • Commercial - the searcher is considering a purchase and wants to investigate their options.
  • Transactional - the searcher wants to purchase something.

Content Strategy

In oder to effectively engage with your audiance and outperform yourcompetition's marketing initiatives, it helps to construct a comprehensive content strategy plan. This plan refers to how youintend to manage your marketing content (i.e. where and when you plan to publish). Your content strategy should include:

  • Your contact format (i.e social media post, articles, etc.)
  • The channels you will publish on (i.e your business websites, social media networks, etc.)
  • How you will manage the content (who will do the posting and manage the customers engagements)
  • Content cunstruction (who will actually do the writing? if outsourcing, what firm will you use?)
  • Content performance (who will analyze how your content is performing and report back to you?)
